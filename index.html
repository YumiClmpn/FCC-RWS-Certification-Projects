Life of Vincent Van Gogh

  • 1853: Vincent van Gogh is born on March 30 in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands.
  • 1869: Begins working at the Goupil & Cie art dealership, which helps develop his interest in art.
  • 1873: Transfers to the London branch of Goupil & Cie.
  • 1875: Van Gogh is transferred to the Paris branch of Goupil & Cie.
  • 1876: Leaves Goupil & Cie and returns to the Netherlands. Begins teaching and preaching in various locations.
  • 1880: Decides to pursue a career as an artist. Begins formal art training in Brussels.
  • 1881: Moves to The Hague, where he experiments with various artistic styles.
  • 1883: Moves to Nuenen, a small town in the Netherlands, where he produces numerous paintings and drawings depicting rural life.
  • 1885: Moves to Antwerp, Belgium, to attend the Royal Academy of Fine Arts. Adopts a darker color palette in his works.
  • 1886: Moves to Paris and becomes acquainted with avant-garde artists, including the Impressionists and Post-Impressionists. Develops his own unique style.
  • 1888: Moves to Arles in the south of France, seeking inspiration from the vibrant colors and light. Begins producing some of his most famous works, including "The Sunflowers" series.
  • 1889: Experiences mental health struggles, including bouts of depression and hallucinations. Cuts off a portion of his ear after a heated argument with artist Paul Gauguin.

  • 1890: Van Gogh dies on July 29, at the age of 37, from a self-inflicted gunshot wound.
